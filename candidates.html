Mia Doona & Kate Poppy

Mia and Kate are third year students on BA drama, applied theatre and education. Have a question? Contact Mia or Kate via email.

Aims for the year
  • Start something new: To ask the student body what new societies they would like to see and work collaboratively in the creation of them
  • Creating communities: Work with other officers on creating events to repair the community within Central
  • Back to normal: To get the societies that existed pre-COVID up and running again
  • Keeping going: Ensure all societies have prolonged success by remaining in close contact with the presidents
  • Spreading the word: Have a key focus on advertising and promoting societies

Hey! We’re Mia and Kate, pronouns both she/her! We’re aware that life at a drama school can be full on, demanding and full of many hours spent slaving away in a studio. We believe there needs to be some respite from this; some time that is spent doing something else that we enjoy, but doing it purely for fun! The societies at Central have the potential to be that respite for students, and we want to make them more of a priority within the school.

Societies are a place where any course and year can come together and dedicate time to something where there is a mutual enjoyment. We are aware that there is often a divide between courses and that was made even more prominent in the last year with the COVID pandemic. Each course had been physically made to stay apart from each other for over a year, and we want to help integrate everybody again. These societies can massively help with getting people to meet each other and create connections between courses. We have seen through working with the football team this year that these societies have the potential to create new connections and friendships, in this instance, between the players and supporters. We want to make sure that this support remains throughout the season, as well as getting the netball team up and running again, and to make sure they have an equal amount of support!

We also recognise that the societies often don’t have much advertisement, therefore not many people know what is on offer and don’t get to make the most of them. We want to make sure everybody knows what is available and we also want students to be able to come to us and propose other societies that they would like; we will be here to support them and help them get started!

Nominated by Felix Kai

Immie Crabtree

Immie is a third year student on BA drama, applied theatre and education. Have a question? Contact Immie via email.

Aims for the year
  • Holding a series of workshops targeting the climate crisis and what role we can all play in saving the planet
  • Creating a safe environment for students to tackle eco-anxiety together
  • Organising events to fight for climate justice, e.g. marches, mini performances, clothes trades
  • Coming together to discuss ways of ending plastic pollution within Central and within our own homes
  • Creating a space for people to reconnect and reignite or develop a passion for the environment!

Hi, I’m Immie and I am super passionate about saving our planet! I know this can sometimes feel hard, or even impossible because it’s easy to feel out of control with the climate crisis. Truth is, although the main issue is with corporate companies primarily burning fossil fuels and dumping plastic into the ocean which have fundamentally increased the concentration of greenhouse gases in Earth's atmosphere, we too can help contribute to the change as individuals. As your environments officer, I aim to tackle to climate emergency with a team of other passionate people in as many ways as we can. This will include events such as eco-friendly learning workshops, clothes trading sessions, quiz nights to test and improve our eco knowledge, guest speaker Q&As, marches/protests, small performances, competitions and more!

I also know that not everyone is an environmental expert, not even I am! However, part of my role would be helping people learn and grow together with a no-judgement, no-pressure and no-obligation approach. Too many people judge others on what they don’t know, or even do know. I am here simply to bring people together and work collectively to do some brilliant things this year. Anybody and everybody will be encouraged to attend the events I aim to create around the year, no matter who you are, how you identify, whether you’re already knowledgeable and passionate about the environment, or whether you know nothing about it. Let’s come together and make a change.

Be the solution to the pollution.

Chloe Bond & Josefine Bruun Meyer

Chloe is a second year student on BA drama, applied theatre and education. Josefine is a second year student on BA stage management. Have a question? Contact Chloe or Josefine via email.

Aims for the year
  • Creating a strong sense of community and network of support amongst Central’s LGBT+ students
  • Regular events, targeting all letters of the acronym. Events open to all students and events exclusively for LGBT+ students to create a safer space – Including entertainment, events not centred around alcohol, workshops, talks and regular meet-ups
  • Making the school and classroom a safer space for LGBT+ students, through campaigns and mandatory training on inclusivity and inclusive language for students and regular staff
  • Working with administration and the Trans Officer to make registration and paperwork more inclusive; including but not limited to pronouns on IDs, Emails & forms, and simpler and safer registration for LGBT+ students

Our main focus this year is to bring back a strong sense of community and network for LGBT+ students, whose mental health statistically has been more negatively impacted by the Covid-19 pandemic, than that of those who do not identify as LGBT+ (Source: KFF). We want to do this through regular events that both include the whole acronym but also target specific groups within the acronym, in order to provide safer spaces. Likewise we have a strong focus on overturning administrative processes and putting pressure on Central to take an active approach to inclusivity. Together, we want to decentre ourselves and use the platform to actively support and hand the mic to our fellow LGBT+ students – as well as listen and learn from our peers.

Hi, I’m Josefine, my pronouns are she/her and I’m a 2nd year Stage Management student. Outside of Central, I’m an editor of Denmark’s only printed LGBT+ magazine, HeartCore, where content contributors get a platform to write about themes and issues that are important to them – not just to the editors of the magazine. I want to transfer this mentality to the role as LGBT+ officer and use the privileges I benefit from to, together with Chloe, create a safer space within Central and within the LGBT+ community at Central for all students. Likewise, I’m very passionate about making stage management more inclusive and actively using my practice to walk the talk, every day.

Hi, I’m Chloe and my main focuses are being a positive and present influence on behalf of the LGBT+ community and to be a consistent voice for change and equality. Also, to be able to provide exciting opportunities and events which enable students to be expressive and creative. With my 3 years’ experience of being president of an LGBT+ society, I am fortunate enough to have been part of a platform that offered support, community, space to express, and fundraising. From this I feel like I have the right foundations, along with Josefine, to push for active encouragement in boosting centrals equality and inclusivity and providing a safer space for all students.

Maya Farmer

Maya is a third year student on BA drama, applied theatre and education. Have a question? Contact Maya via email.

Aims for the year
  • Ensuring that the role is undertaken with intersectionality as a priority, I want to work closely with BAME, Trans and Women’s officers and seek to fight discrimination within the Queer community as well as the school in its entirety
  • Creating a platform for Queer art by implementing more queer cabarets, open mics and scratch nights
  • A focus on education, with workshops on Queer history, positive allyship, and ensuring that all existing workshops are inclusive for the Queer community
  • Creating friendly and accommodating spaces for members of the LGBTQ+ community to be together
  • Creating a Queer community that exists outside of Central

As LGBTQ+ officer I wish to listen, learn and fight for voices to be heard, education and growth are paramount to creating an intersectional LGBTQ+ community at Central. With this being my priority, I aim to work as closely as possible with BAME, Trans and Women’s officers to ensure that intersectionality is seen as a leading force within my practice as LGBTQ+ officer and the community as a whole. Through working with other officers, I want to make the pre-existing workshops at Central inclusive and informative for LGBTQ+ students. The Queer community is not innocent of malpractice, and I want to ensure that consent modules include specific nuances that only exist within LGBTQ+ relationships. In addition to this, I would hold allyship workshops and workshops on specific issues within the LGBTQ+ community e.g., biphobia to ensure that all members of the LGBTQ+ community at Central feel supported and heard.

The LGBTQ+ community does not disappear into the abyss the morning after the fresher’s queer cabaret. We are a large and wonderful part of the student body that deserve to be celebrated and facilitated throughout the entirety of our studies at Central. I want to make events such as the Queer Cabaret a recurring part of the student union nightlife at the university, I want to hold a Queer Cabaret and an open mic once a month (meaning an event would take place every two weeks) not only to give performers the opportunity to showcase their talent, but to give other students a space to socialise within a positive and uplifting atmosphere.

However, Queer social life should not be limited to nights out and evening shows/events, I also want to implement other events/groups that are not centred around night life. For example, I would like to facilitate an LGBTQ+ book club, the ability to get together and talk about being queer and our own and other stories is just as valuable to the community as a safe and fun night out. I would also collaborate with BAME officers to arrange exclusively BAME Queer socials.

I also will be pushing to have some spaces facilitated for the creation of art and performance, I want to hold regular: painting nights, writing lunches, scratch nights, improv workshops etc for Queer people to get together and share ideas and work on whatever they want to without the pressures of assessment or expectation.

I would like to create and sustain a community within central that also supports each other outside of uni. My aim is to create an atmosphere in which, if a student wants to go to, for example, a space where they would feel more comfortable if supported by another queer person, they know somebody at Central that could go with them.

I do not want queer belonging and solidarity to begin and end with facilitated workshops, I want to create an organic community that sustains itself and is a constant source of support both during and after student’s time at Central.

I will be a friendly and approachable face around the university, and I will be open to talk to you about anything, to be on your side and fight in your corner on whatever issues may arise.

Trans people often feel, or are made to feel, that because they are a minority, they have no voice. I want to challenge this and create a community at Central where we feel supported in raising any problems we might encounter as trans students. I would like to create a group for trans and non-binary people to meet and get to know each other.
