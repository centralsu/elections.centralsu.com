Officer roles for election

Find out more about the roles we’re looking to fill at the October by-election.

Activities officer

Activities is the name; societies are the game. You’re the point of contact for anyone running or thinking or starting a club, society or sports team at Central.

You’ll be responsible – with the president – for allocating funds and resources to societies in a fair and impartial way and making sure society leaders are clear about their responsibilities to students and the union when using our funds.

Environments officer

You’re our head honcho for matters of environmental awareness.

LGBT+ officer

Join your fellow freshers for the long-awaited re-opening of the backstage bar (with free fizz). Second and third-years welcome too.

Trans officer

Join your fellow freshers for the long-awaited re-opening of the backstage bar (with free fizz). Second and third-years welcome too.

Vice president for acting

As the primary representative of acting students at Central, you’ll ensure all union departments are considerate of the needs and wishes of those you represent, and work to make their voices heard by the school. As well as showcasing the work of your peers, you’re responsible for making sure they get the information they need from the union throughout their studies.

As a member of the executive committee, you’ll be involved in the inner workings of the union and deputise for the president at their request.

Vice president for postgraduates

As the ultimate representative of all students on postgraduate courses, you’ll ensure all union departments are considerate of the needs and wishes of those you represent, and work to make their voices heard by the school. You’re responsible for making sure all postgraduate students get the information they need from the union throughout their studies.

As a member of the executive committee, you’ll be involved in the inner workings of the union and deputise for the president at their request.

This role is a student governor. You will be co-opted onto the board of governors and become a director of the company.
