Events officer

Organsing activities to support students.

You arrange regular events in the backstage bar and around the school based on feedback from students. Freshers and summer ball are your busiest times, when you’ll lead the planning, and you’ll be in contact with the ALCOPA group to organise events with other London conservatoires.

You’ll work with society leaders to organise sports and societies. This includes managing the societies budget and allocating it fairly with the vice-president.

Being outgoing and full of ideas is a key strength and being organised and able to manage your time is always important.

Union links: Society leaders

Staff links: Bar manager • director of operations • estates team

Committees: All London colleges of performing arts

Find out more
We’ll pay you a stipend. You’ll receive a small salary of between £1,000 and £2,000 per year to recognise the work you do for the union, subject to approval.

Inclusion officer

Making everyone welcome in our community.

You’re dedicated to improving the experience of students from marginalised communities.

You’ll work with the inclusion office to develop the new inclusion rep system to make sure everyone’s voice is heard by the union and by Central.

Being good at listening to and understanding people’s experiences and positionalities will be key. You’re not expected to be a caseworker – this role is about making things better for everyone at Central by sharing student experiences with the school.

Union links: Inclusion reps

Staff links: Director of learning, teaching and inclusion • EDI manager

Committees: Inclusion committee • diversity and inclusion committee • conservatoires UK EDI group

Find out more
We’ll pay you a stipend. You’ll receive a small salary of between £1,000 and £2,000 per year to recognise the work you do for the union, subject to approval.

Postgraduate officer

Looking out for postgraduate students’ academic interests.

You’re the ultimate representative of postgraduate students and researchers.

Working with the faculty office and undergraduate officer, you’ll amplify student feedback and make sure Central hears it by promoting student feedback mechanisms. This will include developing course committee as courses are restructured.

Union links: Course reps

Staff links: Vice-principal (curriculum and students) • vice-principal (research and knowledge exchange) • academic registrar • heads of academic departments

Committees: Governing body • academic board • learning, teaching and student experience committee • course committees

The postgraduate officer is also a student governor. You’ll be co-opted onto Central’s board of governors, becoming a director of the company and trustee of the charity.
We’re not electing a postgraduate officer. They’ll be elected in October, when the current vice-president for postgraduates finishes.

President

Leading the work of the union day-to-day.

You are the ultimate representative of students at Central, representing them at committees and events. You’ll work full-time in the union, managing its day-to-day operations and supporting the officer team.

You’ll lead regular team meetings as well as sitting on a number of school committees and liaising with external groups like CUKSN, ALCOPA and NUS.

Experience leading a team will be helpful, as will confidence speaking publicly in committees or at events. You’ll need to be a quick learner to adapt yourself to the many parts of the role – from budgeting to event promotion, risk assessment to charity governance – and every day can bring new challenges. Being able to manage your time effectively while being pulled in different directions will be essential.

Union links: Student council • board of trustees

Staff links: Principal • deputy principal • vice-principals • directors • heads of academic departments

Committees: Governing body • diversity and inclusion committee • academic board • inclusion committee • health and safety management committee • and many more…

Find out more
This is a full-time job. If you’re not graduating, you’ll need to take a break in studies for the year.
The president is also a trustee of the union and an ex-officio governor. You’ll be made a member of Central’s board of governors, becoming a director of the company and trustee of the charity.

Undergraduate officer

Looking out for undergraduate students’ academic interests.

You’re all about the educational experience of undergraduates, representing their views to senior committees at Central. You’ll work with course reps to support them in their role.

Working with the faculty office and postgraduate officer, you’ll amplify student feedback and make sure Central hears it by promoting student feedback mechanisms. This will include developing course committee as courses are restructured.

The ability to listen to feedback and know where to take it will be helpful. Confidence when speaking to academic staff or chairing meetings will be useful.

Vice-president

Making the work of the union possible.

This is a senior role in the union. You’ll work closely with the president behind the scenes to make everything the union does possible and successful. You might need to deputise for the president, representing the union at events and committees.

Not all your work is hidden though – you also lead the student council, chairing its meetings and ensuring the democratic voice of students is heard.

Maybe you have experience in logistics, or just enjoy the challenge of organising people and projects. An interest in governance and democracy would be particularly useful, and knowledge of how the union works would be helpful.

Union links: Student council • board of trustees

Staff links: Deputy principal • vice-principals • directors • heads of academic departments • head of governance and executive support
