About inclusion reps

We’ve removed the rigid officer roles which have made it hard for some communities to feel heard. We know groups like carers, mature learners and Jewish students haven’t been properly represented in the past, so we’re making it easier for people to speak to their identity and positions to better inform the union’s work.

We’ve spent months talking to you, gathering your thoughts and discussing different structures. Our new structure allows for more involvement from you and for a close relationship with the union. Central supports the changes we’ve made to help the union develop and become more effective at our mission – making life better for students.

We think inclusion reps should be volunteers who nominate themselves, rather than being elected. Elections are a powerful symbol but often become a popularity contest. They also make it difficult to express your identity as you want, because we’d have to come up with a list of roles you could stand for, automatically excluding some groups.

You can find out much more about what’s changed and why on our section of the student handbook.
